HANDLING & STORAGE

DO’S

  • Store indoors. If left outside, cover it with a waterproof covering.
  • If a stack or bundle gets soaked in rain; separate the sheets immediately. Wipe off the water with a cloth and dry it thoroughly prior to stacking it again.
  • Check the stock frequently and ensure it stays dry.
  • While handling sheets, use personal protective equipment such as hand gloves and closed shoes.
  • While storing, maintain a slope of minimum 50 deg; this will drain the rainwater which may penetrate the waterproof cover.
  • For long-time storage, stack the sheets neatly and place them horizontally over the ground. Ensure at least 30 cm of gap between the ground and stack.
  • Carefully handle the sheets while placing them to not get damaged.

DONT’S

  • Never keep heavy materials stacked over sheets, as it may deform sheets.
  • Avoid direct contact with soil.
  • Never store sheets beside walkways.
  • Avoid dragging sheets over other sheets or rough surfaces; carry them instead.
  • Metal chains are not suitable for lifting sheets.
  • Avoid keeping sheets vertically without proper support.
  • Avoid the sheet surface from getting wet.

INSTALLATION

DO’S

  • Never cut sheets/materials placing over sheets. Hot materials can damage the coating of sheets.
  • Clean the debris after drilling, as it can cause rust.
  • Neutral cure rubber sealants can be used as sealant material.
  • Ensure proper fastening procedures are followed during installation.
  • It’s recommended to clean the roof with clean water or mild detergent using a soft cloth/mop or soft nylon bristle brush to avoid swarf stain and rusts.
  • Paint the structural fixtures such as pipes, angles, truss, channels before sheet installation. Zinc-coated supporting structures don’t require painting.
  • Don’t fix prepainted galvalume & prepainted galvanized products directly over a cemented surface, as the lime content in cement will accelerate the corrosion.
  • Ensure the rubber washer of self-drilling screws are roofing/walling material compliant.

DONT’S

  • Never leave metal filings over sheets.
  • Avoid direct contact with cement.
  • Avoid installation during rainy days, as sheets can get wet and slippery.
  • Refrain from walking over single rib sheet, end laps gutter, and skylight sheets.
  • Debris over sheets should be avoided.
  • Ensure the groove portion doesn’t come outside. Improper installation can cause water leakage.
  • Don’t use abrasive or solvent-type clean and wire brushes, steel wool to clean the roofing sheet, as it damages the paint coating.
  • Avoid the use of acetic acid-based sealants, as they emit aggressive by-products during curing.
  • Don’t use your sheeted roof as a bird feeding ground, as the bird droppings can damage the aesthetics and color of the sheet.
  • Avoid the use of stainless-steel screws, j-hack, and carbon washers, as they may damage the sheet.
  • Avoid accumulation of dirt over the sheet.
